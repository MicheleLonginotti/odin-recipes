Gin Fizz

The Gin Fizz is a classic cocktail renowned for its light, refreshing, and effervescent qualities. Originating in the late 19th century, this drink combines gin, fresh citrus, sugar, and carbonated water to create a balanced blend of botanical, sweet, and tart flavors. The inclusion of egg white in some variations adds a creamy texture and a frothy head, enhancing the overall mouthfeel and presentation of the cocktail. Shaken vigorously, the ingredients emulsify and integrate, resulting in a smooth and invigorating beverage.

Historically popular in America, especially during the Jazz Age, the Gin Fizz became a staple in bars and has maintained its status as a timeless cocktail. Its simplicity and versatility have led to numerous adaptations, but the classic recipe remains a favorite for those seeking a crisp and revitalizing drink. Whether sipped at brunch or enjoyed as an evening refresher, the Gin Fizz continues to delight cocktail enthusiasts around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz (60 ml) gin
  • 1 oz (30 ml) fresh lemon juice
  • 3/4 oz (22 ml) simple syrup
  • Club soda
  • Ice cubes
  • (Optional) 1 egg white
  • (Optional) Lemon slice or twist for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Measure out the gin, fresh lemon juice, and simple syrup. If using, separate one egg white.
  2. Combine in Shaker: Add the gin, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white (if using) to a cocktail shaker.
  3. Dry Shake: Secure the shaker lid and shake vigorously without ice for about 10 seconds to emulsify the egg white.
  4. Add Ice and Shake Again: Add ice cubes to the shaker and shake thoroughly for another 15 seconds until well-chilled.
  5. Strain into Glass: Strain the mixture into a chilled highball or Collins glass without ice.
  6. Top with Club Soda: Gently pour club soda (about 2-3 oz or 60-90 ml) into the glass to create the fizz and fill the glass.
  7. Garnish and Serve: Add a lemon slice or twist on top for garnish if desired. Serve immediately and enjoy.
