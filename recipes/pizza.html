Pizza Margherita

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 25 mins Total: 35 mins Rating: 5 stars

Pizza Margherita is a classic Neapolitan-style pizza named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, who visited Naples in 1889. The story goes that a local baker created a dish resembling the Italian flag, with tomato sauce (red), mozzarella (white), and fresh basil leaves (green). The flavors of this simple yet elegant pizza are perfectly balanced and have become a staple of Italian cuisine.

Pizza Margherita is a must-have dish when visiting Naples, but it can also be easily prepared at home. The key is to use fresh ingredients, such as San Marzano tomatoes, high-quality mozzarella, and fragrant basil leaves. The crust should be crispy and slightly charred, with a delicate crunch that pairs perfectly with the flavorful toppings. Whether you're in Naples or not, a well-made Pizza Margherita is a culinary experience that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese (sliced)
  • San Marzano tomatoes (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat oven to 550 F (290 C) with a pizza stone inside, if you have one.
  2. Shape Pizza Dough: Punch down pizza dough and shape into a ball. Then flatten it into a disk using a rolling pin or your hands.
  3. Top Pizza: Place the pizza dough on a piece of parchment paper or a lightly floured pizza peel. Spread tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a 1/2 inch border around the edges. Top with mozzarella slices and fresh basil leaves.
  4. Bake Pizza: Slide the parchment paper or pizza peel onto the preheated stone and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until crust is golden and cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for 2-3 minutes. Slice and serve immediately, garnished with extra basil leaves and mozzarella if desired.
